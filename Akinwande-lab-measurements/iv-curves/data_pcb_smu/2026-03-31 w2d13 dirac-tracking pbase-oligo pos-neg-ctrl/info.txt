The results look bad, all channels follow the same FLAT trend between the pos, neg, and blank ctrl. The functionalization looked all even this time, which is good. Notably, the functionalization does not look good after the PBASE step. there is not P-doping after the oligos are incubated. This leads me to believe that the oligo are not attaching to the NHS ester on the PBASE. It is good that all the wells are FLAT because if there is capture oligo, then the baseline is establishe well and all the unbound oligos are washing off effectively. What I think went wrong:

(1) I think the NHS ester on the PBASE hydrolyzed. The halflife of NHS ester is 10-30 mins in 1X PBS and the dirac measurment took ~15 mins between the PBASE and oligo incubation. Also the half life of NHS ester decreases as the pH of 1X PBS goes up.

To fix this, 
(1) I will be using store-bought 1X PBS instead of the self-made tablet 1X PBS, since this controls the pH more. I could have had a super high pH and had no idea, contributing to the hydrolization. 
(2) I will be skipping the dirac measurement step between the PBASE and the oligo measurement to decrease the time that NHS ester is in contact with 1X PBS. The 15 mins to measure could be killing it. I won't have this characterization, which is bad because I won't be able to track the doping, but this is necessary to eliminate this factor. I can try to see the final shift to confirm attachment, or look at the concentration curves (obviously)
